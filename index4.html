The Isle of Dragons

Chapter Four

As they climbed higher into the air, Fran twisted to look back at the pursuers coming upon the cave. A volley of arrows whistled close by but thankfully missed both her and the dragon. She just had time to glimpse Tarm, staring at her open-mouthed. She saw him whirl around and shout a warning, appearing to stop the archers from loosing another volley of arrows into the air. “Thank you Tarm.” she thought to herself.

✥

“Are they your enemies? Should I return and destroy them? It would be quite easy for me.” the voice in her head spoke out suddenly.

“No, no! They are my people! Don’t hurt them, they think you aim to destroy them.” she quickly responded, desperately hoping the dragon would heed her plea.

“Very well little one, I have no wish to make more war,” the dragon said in a gentle tone. “We are safe now.”

✥

Fran realised that they were in the cloud. She could not see further than a few feet ahead and moisture clung to her as they flew up and up. The wind filled her ears with a rushing sound as they climbed and then, quite suddenly, they burst through the cloud into a clear sky. The sun beamed down on them as they flew over what looked like a sea of cotton wool

✥

“It’s so beautiful!” she thought to herself, marvelling at this sight that none of her people had witnessed before.

“The sky is as much my home as the earth,” said Aryzath. “We travel now to a sacred place that few of your kind know of.”

“What kind of place? Where is it?” asked Fran, suddenly afraid of where this creature might take her.

“Have no fear, little friend, you will soon see all” the dragon’s voice soothed her.

✥

They flew on for the rest of the day. Fran began to grow tired and every now and then she felt her grip on the dragon’s scaly neck slip. If she fell asleep she was sure she would fall to her death. As this thought settled uncomfortably in her mind, something caught her eye on the horizon. As Aryzath beat his wings, they drew closer to what appeared to be a green object in the sky. Soon it loomed larger and Fran could make out the details of it. It was some kind of vast island, floating in the sky!

✥

“This cannot be! How can this be?” her mind reeled with wonder at the sight. She could see green hills rolling into the distant centre of the island, where lofty peaks could be seen. Rivers and streams ran down from the heights. It looked like a paradise. What was this place, floating so impossibly in the sky?

✥

As they gently touched down, she slid from her place on the dragon and touched the fragrant grass beneath her feet. This was not possible. It could not be true.

“This is the sanctuary place. Forbidden to most. Only a special few may tread here. For thousands of years it was home to the bonded pairs. Dragon and human, joined together in a mystical bond. No others can tread here lest the gods sweep them away,” Aryzath pawed the ground with his great claw. “I myself have never trodden the ground here for fear of that. None of my kind have in living memory”. His eyes stared into hers, willing her to realise what was happening between them.

✥

As Fran stared at the dragon, she knew that this was the start of something she had unknowingly wanted all of her short life. She was now bonded with this dragon and the possibilities began to fill her mind. She could see the future that awaited them both, a life of wonder and adventure. There were old tales amongst her people of the dragon riders, but these were considered to be myth, fairy tales made up for the entertainment of children. Now she knew that to be a lie. Like all myths, this one had a grain of truth. She was now a dragon rider.
