The Isle of Dragons

Chapter Three

The next few hours passed like a dream for Fran. Before she knew what she was doing, she was kneeling alongside the great creature, staring into its eyes. Fran listened with her mind as the dragon told her his story as if they were old friends.

❋

His name was Aryzath and was one of the last of his clan. His homeland was far to the west and his folk had lived there for thousands of years until an enemy dragon clan attacked their home in great numbers. His folk were caught unprepared and were massacred by the enemy dragons. All the young dragons and the elders were killed. The warriors were defeated and scattered to the winds.

❋

Aryzath was a captain of the warrior breed of his clan and fought until he was too wounded to continue. He expected to be killed by his enemies but they simply left him for dead, bleeding in the ocean. He drifted on the seas until he regained consciousness and found himself here at the Misty Isles. Confused and disorientated, he had laid waste to the land in his rage, searching for his enemies until he realised he was not in his homelands any longer. Exhausted by the effort and his wounds, he had retreated to this cave to recover and consider his next path.

❋

“I have no honour now, young one” he said. “I am defeated, lost, and without a home or folk to call my own. If you have come to slay me, then I pray you do so quickly. I am wretched now and do not deserve life.” Aryzath’s eyes closed and a great sighing breath left his body. “I have no desire to harm you or your people. My ears are still keen though, and I hear others of your folk approaching from the East. I know what humans do to my kind. They kill or enslave. I know not what fate awaits me with them. Death is the most preferable of the two. Please young one, use your weapon now.”

❋

“I carry no weapons, dragon,” said Fran quietly. “Although my people will be armed. They have come to tame you or destroy you if you cannot be taken.” Fran was deep in thought. She should take this dragon and try to capture or destroy him like the rest of her folk. Is this not what she had wanted when she set out on this journey? Something in her heart had changed after hearing this dragon’s tale. Somehow a shift had happened and now she could not face the idea of this magnificent creature being captured or destroyed. She made her decision.

❋

“Come, dragon. We must flee now or you will indeed face death. I do not think you will allow yourself to be captured and that means certain death for you. My heart tells me that your fate is not to die here. There is still more for you to do.” The dragon stirred at these words. “There is something you should know, girl. Humans are rarely able to converse with dragonkind. This is a remarkable thing. This means you are a dragon-friend, a special type of human blessed by the Gods of dragons and humans both. It was no mere chance that we met here.”

❋

The dragon roused himself and moved gracefully out of the cave. “Come then. If we are to go, you must accompany me. Seat yourself upon my back, just where the neck becomes the shoulder. There you will be safe and comfortable.” Fran, taken aback by this information, opened her eyes wide and stared at the dragon for a moment before quickly gathering her things. Very carefully she got into position on the dragon’s shoulders. Aryzath turned his head to look at her for a moment before snorting and launching himself high into the air. His body plunged over the side of the mountain dropping like a stone until his wings unfurled and he beat the air strongly, rising rapidly towards the clouds. Fran held on to his neck tightly, trying not to scream with fear.
